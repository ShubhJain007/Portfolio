Shubh Jain

I am Shubh, a graduate student at the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), pursuing a Master of Science in Robotic Systems Development. I am passionate about working at the intersection of computer vision, DL/ML, and controls in robotics.

During my undergraduate studies, I dedicated countless hours to designing and building Fully Autonomous Underwater Vehicles for RoboSub from scratch. Additionally, my coursework and projects in areas like image processing, control systems, machine learning, and mechanical design provided a strong foundation that I further advanced through their application in AUVs and my internships.

Beyond academia and robotics projects, I find joy in calisthenics, sketching, playing the flute, and Ultimate Frisbee.

Adaptive Underwater Image Restoration and Object Detection and Tracking

This project implements adaptive image restoration techniques with object detection and tracking. It is optimized for lightweight embedded devices and uses semantic segmentation and cross-correlation-based trackers.

Autonomous Underwater Vehicle - Anahita

Contains ROS packages developed for our team’s autonomous underwater vehicle "Anahita", prepared specifically for the Singapore Autonomous Underwater Vehicle Challenge 2024. This repository includes the complete ROS software stack for control, perception, navigation, and simulation, along with sensor integration modules.

View on GitHub

Enhancing Real-Time Lip-Sync and Avatar Quality for Online Meetings

This project presents an AI-driven pipeline for creating realistic, real-time avatars tailored for online meetings and virtual communication. The system combines low-light video enhancement (using LIME, DUAL, and Zero-DCE), lip-sync generation (with MuseTalk), and face restoration (via CodeFormer) to deliver sharp, expressive, and natural-looking avatars. Optimized for real-time performance, it ensures seamless lip synchronization and high visual quality even under challenging lighting conditions.

View on GitHub

Dynamic Vehicle Routing Problem (DVRP) for paratransit services

This project is a re-implementation of An Online Approach to Solve the Dynamic Vehicle Routing Problem with Stochastic Trip Requests for Paratransit Services. It was developed as part of the course Embedded Cyber-Physical Systems (CS-637) at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, India, focusing on efficient real-time routing strategies for demand-responsive transport systems.
